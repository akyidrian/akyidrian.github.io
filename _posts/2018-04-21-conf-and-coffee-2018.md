Last weekend I attended *Conf & Coffee 2018* organised by [Code & Coffee][codeAndCoffee] at the [Unbounce][unbounce] offices in Vancouver. As the names suggests, this was a conference for coders with a taste for coffee. There was roughly an 80/20 split of professionals to students from quite varied backgrounds based on my interactions, although most appeared to gravitate towards mobile and web development. The event was just over 2 days (14th and 15th of April) and cost me CAD$50 for a professional ticket.

As a keyword blowout summary, the technical talks and workshops were based around React, Node, JavaScript, Ruby, CSS, Elm, Elixir, Haskell, Blockchain, Python, Arduino, Raspberry Pi, Johnny-Five and Toccata. So some of it was familiar and plenty was foreign to me. There were also more general conversations around data infrastructure, functional programming, software development, leadership, mentorship, project management and coffee (of course). A complete conference schedule can be found [here][confAndCoffeeSchedule].

In the end I personally spent most of my time in workshops because there is only so much one can listen before an urge to do something sets in. To provide some more colour, on Saturday I attended these:
1. What Makes a Great Developer
2. Building a React Component Library
3. Computing for Fusion Reactor (General Fusion)
4. JavaScript - The weird and not-so-weird parts
5. Build and Code with Raspberry Pi - ***Workshop***

and on Sunday I attended these:
1. 10 Mistakes You'll Make as a New Lead
2. NodeBots - ***Workshop***
3. Learn Elixir & Build a Real Time Chat App - ***Workshop***
4. Working Backwards: Outcomes & Goals

Overall my experience was positive and the event ran smoothly despite it being the first conference organised by this group (as far as I know). Lots of people attended on Saturday (~80 as an estimate) but there was a large dropout on Sunday presumably because 7 days of code was too much for most. There was a good variation in food on both days, which is always a big deal! There were also tons of stickers flying around too and I managed to score an Arduino Uno from the NodeBots workshop!

My top 5 main take-aways from the conference were:
* Engineers and the software systems they create are inherently coupled
* JavaScript is weird
* Electronics and JavaScript apparently do mix
* I really need to learn functional programming
* Leadership skills can be learnt and must be learnt even by those who aren't in leadership roles

I hope to create more detailed posts on these take-aways soon so stay tuned!
